Royal Council

Council of Oro Traditional Rulers (C.O.T.R)

The Council of Oro Traditional Rulers (C.O.T.R) is the custodial arm of the Oron Union, comprising the paramount and royal rulers of the Oro Nation. The C.O.T.R provides traditional wisdom, cultural authority and moral guidance to the Central Executive Committee, ensuring that all decisions of the Union respect ancestral custom and the spiritual heritage of the Oro people. Their presence anchors the Union in its deepest roots.

How We Are Organised

Organisational Structure

The Oron Union is structured across five interconnected tiers — from the Central Executive Committee at the apex to the clans and village units at the grassroots — united under one constitution and one purpose.

1

Central Executive Committee (CEC)

The CEC is the supreme elected administrative body of the Oron Union, responsible for day-to-day governance, policy implementation, and representing the Union at all levels. It is elected biennially at the General Assembly.

The Wings of the Union

Oron Union Youth Wing
OUYW

The vanguard of the Union's future, the Youth Wing mobilises Oro youth in leadership, community service, sports and entrepreneurship. It operates branches in all five LGAs and across diaspora chapters.

Focus Areas: Leadership Development • Scholarships • Sports • Entrepreneurship • Cultural Advocacy
Youth Wing President: Mr. Effiong Okon Bassey
Oron Union Women's Wing
OUWW

The Women's Wing champions the rights, welfare and economic empowerment of Oro women at home and in the diaspora. It coordinates market cooperatives, health outreaches and educational grants for girls.

Focus Areas: Economic Empowerment • Health • Girls' Education • Cultural Preservation • Welfare
Women's Wing President: Mrs. Ekaette Nse Effiong
Oro Student Union
OSU

The Oro Student Union unites Oro students across Nigerian universities and polytechnics, providing academic support, bursaries, mentorship, and a platform for cultural identity on campuses nationwide.

Focus Areas: Academic Excellence • Bursaries • Campus Identity • Graduate Mentorship • Career Support
OSU President: Comr. Aniedi Bassey Okon

Those Who Came Before

Past Presidents-General

The Oron Union has been shaped by great men who gave their best years in service to the Oro people. We honour their memory and build on their legacies.

1925 – 1940
Chief Effiong Nwa Okon — Founding President

The visionary founder who convened the first meeting of the Oron Union in 1925, establishing the constitutional framework that has guided the organisation for a century. Under his leadership the Union gained the support of all five LGA communities.

1941 – 1956
Chief Bassey Atai Effiong — Constitutional Foundation

Presided over the drafting and adoption of the Union's first formal constitution, transforming what had been a loose assembly of elders into a structured, rule-governed democratic organisation capable of long-term planning.

1957 – 1972
Chief Okon Edet Akpan — Independence Era

Led the Union through the transition to Nigerian independence and the turbulent Civil War years, preserving Union assets and membership records and ensuring that the organisation emerged intact and stronger than before.

1973 – 1988
Dr. Nse Okon Effiong — State Creation Champion

Spearheaded the Oron Union's campaign for the creation of Akwa Ibom State, lobbying federal authorities and mobilising Oro communities across the country. His success in 1987 remains one of the Union's greatest political achievements.

1989 – 2007
Chief Asuquo Ekpo Nwa — Modern Reforms

Presided over the most sweeping constitutional reforms in Union history, introducing transparent ballot-based elections, financial audits, and the creation of the Board of Trustees as an independent oversight body.

2008 – 2023
Chief Emmanuel Bassey Obot — Digital Transformation

Modernised Union operations by introducing digital membership records, online General Assembly participation, an official website, and the establishment of international chapters in Australia and Germany — bringing the Union firmly into the 21st century.
