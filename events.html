Annual Festivals

Three celebrations that define the Oro cultural calendar

Obon Festival

Held annually in August

The Obon Festival is the Oro Nation's most celebrated cultural event, featuring masquerades, traditional music, royal durbars, and communal feasting. It honours ancestral spirits and strengthens bonds among Oro people at home and in the diaspora.

Uduk Isong Harvest

Held annually in July

The Uduk Isong Harvest Festival is a thanksgiving celebration rooted in Oro agrarian traditions. Communities present first fruits to the elders, accompanied by traditional songs, folk dances and communal prayer for continued prosperity and abundance.

Founders Day Congress

Held annually on May 23

Every 23rd of May, the Oron Union gathers to commemorate the founding of the Union in 1925. Founders Day is an occasion for reflection, the presentation of annual awards, and the renewal of commitment to the Union's mission by all members.
