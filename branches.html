Branches & Chapters

From the five LGAs of Akwa Ibom to cities across Nigeria and the diaspora — the Oron Union is everywhere our people are.

Oron LGA Branch Est. 1925 HQ

Oron, Akwa Ibom State — Founding Branch of the Oron Union

The Oron LGA Branch is the founding and headquarters branch of the Oron Union, established at the Union's inception in 1925. It oversees affairs for the heartland of the Oro Nation, including Oron City, the Oron Museum district and surrounding communities.

Urueoffong / Oruko Branch Est. 1928

Urueoffong/Oruko, Akwa Ibom State

The Urueoffong/Oruko Branch serves the communities of the second largest Oro LGA, known for its rich fishing heritage and historic Oku Ibom waterways that have sustained communities for centuries.

Okobo, Akwa Ibom State

The Okobo Branch serves the agricultural heartland of the Oro Nation, an LGA with strong traditional governance structures, vibrant weekly markets and deep-rooted community leadership.

Mbo Branch Est. 1932

Mbo, Akwa Ibom State

The Mbo Branch represents this coastal community renowned for traditional boat-building, deep-sea fishing and the celebrated Mbo annual regatta. The branch is also known for producing outstanding Oron Union officers across generations.

Udunguko, Akwa Ibom State

The newest Oro LGA branch, established in 1996 following the creation of Udunguko LGA. Celebrated for its abundant agricultural produce and its emerging generation of dynamic youth leaders who have become a driving force in the Union.

Domestic Branches

Oron Union branches serving Oro communities across Nigerian cities

Lagos Branch

Lagos, Nigeria
Est. 1958

The largest domestic branch, serving the Oro community in Lagos and its environs. Hosts monthly meetings and coordinates welfare activities for members in Lagos State.

Abuja Branch

Abuja, FCT, Nigeria
Est. 1991

The federal capital branch, serving Oro professionals and public servants resident in Abuja. Maintains active government liaison for Union advocacy work.

Port Harcourt Branch

Port Harcourt, Rivers State
Est. 1965

Serving the Oro community in Port Harcourt and Rivers State. One of the most active domestic branches with a strong youth and professional membership base.

Kano Branch

Kano, Kano State
Est. 1978

The Northern Nigeria branch, serving Oro traders, professionals and academics in Kano and surrounding states. A testament to the Union's pan-Nigerian reach.

International Chapters

Our global diaspora network — the Oron Union wherever the tide carries our people

🇺🇸

USA Chapter

United States of America
Est. 1999

With chapters in Houston, Atlanta, New York and Washington D.C., the USA is home to the largest Oron Union diaspora community outside Nigeria.

🇬🇧

UK Chapter

United Kingdom
Est. 2003

Based in London with members across England, Scotland and Wales. The UK Chapter hosts the popular annual Oro Cultural Night and coordinates diaspora welfare initiatives.

🇨🇦

Canada Chapter

Canada
Est. 2007

Primarily centred in Toronto and Calgary, serving Oro professionals and students resident in Canada. Known for its active cultural education programmes for Oro children.

🇩🇪

Germany Chapter

Germany
Est. 2012

Serving the growing Oro community in Germany, particularly in Hamburg and Frankfurt. The chapter is known for its strong academic and professional network.

🇦🇺

Australia Chapter

Australia
Est. 2015

The newest international chapter, with members primarily in Sydney and Melbourne. A young and vibrant chapter that has grown rapidly since its founding.
